## 1. The behaviour, and its hard boundary

"Find faces in the room and follow them." On this body that means **head-tracking**:
keep a face centered by panning/tilting. The body has **2 DOF** ‚Äî
`foot` (pan, ¬±90¬∞) and `neck` (tilt, ‚àí60‚Ä¶+35¬∞) ‚Äî and **no locomotion**
([brain/schemas.ts] `DEGREE_LIMITS`). So:

- ‚úÖ pan/tilt to keep a face centered; search-sweep when none; lock on when found;
  re-acquire from last-known position when lost ("search meaningfully").
- ‚úÖ follow a *named* person (face-api recognition) or the most-salient face.
- ‚ùå **pursue** someone who leaves pan range or the room ‚Äî can only search + wait.
  (Locomotion is node-rover, a different device.)

This is an **action** behaviour (goal = move the body), unlike the curator (goal =
maintain memory). Per the purpose-rule it's a genuine behaviour, not pipeline work.

## 3. What must be built

### 3a. A faster face-box source (medium)
Identity samples at `IDENTITY_PERIOD_MS=2000` (2 s) **and is change-debounced** ‚Äî far
too slow + sparse for a tracking loop. The box *data* exists; the *cadence* doesn't.
faceFollow needs boxes at **~4‚Äì5 Hz**. Options: (a) a dedicated faster `recognizeAll`
read for tracking (cheap; competes with vision for the shared frame grabber), or (b) a
box-only fast detector tap (no recognition/expression ‚Äî lighter, smoother). Start with
(a); measure jitter on the real dock before building (b).

### 3b. The controller (medium, well-understood ‚Äî NOT fragile)
Pure-ish arithmetic + a state machine, testable like the curator's logic:
- **box-center error ‚Üí `foot`/`neck` deltas**, with **deadband** (no twitch when ~centered)
  and **slew limit** (glide, not lurch); map normalized error ‚Üí degrees within limits.
- **state machine**: `SEARCH` (sweep) ‚Üí `TRACK` (center) ‚Üí `REACQUIRE` (lost ‚Üí sweep
  from **last-known** foot/neck outward, then full sweep) ‚Üí back. Holds last-known pos.
- **target policy**: most-salient (largest√ócentered) or a named person (recognition);
  build salient first, named is a filter on top.
- **loop-closure**: every move ALSO calls `pushCommand` (3 above), in lockstep.

## 4. The actuator lease (the keystone)

**The problem.** `MotionExecutor` is today "the body's single master, last-write-wins."
Its callers, all unmediated: the brain `move` tool + `set_face`‚Üí`playGesture`
([brain/tools.ts]), tasks' `runSteps` ([tasks/register-capabilities.ts]), the bodylink
console ([bodylink/index.ts]), and the session lifecycle's `stop()` on turn start/cancel
([brain/session.ts]). The moment faceFollow *also* drives the body **continuously**, N
masters fight: last-write-wins becomes thrash, and a crashed follower can freeze the body
mid-pan. So we put a **lease** in front of the executor ‚Äî the one new piece of real
infrastructure.

**Decision: a PRIORITY lease (not "brain always wins").** A caller acquires the body at
a **priority**; the highest active priority holds it; equal priority = last-write-wins
(today's behaviour, preserved). Chosen over "brain always preempts" because it's strictly
more general ‚Äî a future safety/emergency behaviour can outrank even the brain ‚Äî at the
cost of a small amount more machinery. Indicative levels (open, tune in build):

```
  emergency / safety        100   (future: e.g. "stop, about to fall")
  brain turn / explicit move  60   (a conversation gesture, a user `move` command)
  faceFollow (tracking)       30   (the reflex ‚Äî yields to the brain, outranks idle)
  console / manual sliders    20
  idle / heartbeat             0
```

So: a brain turn (60) **preempts** faceFollow (30) ‚Äî faceFollow yields, the brain
gestures, then faceFollow resumes when the brain releases. An emergency (100) outranks
even the brain.

**The lease contract** (what the executor grows):
- `acquire(dock, holder, priority) ‚Üí lease | null` ‚Äî granted iff priority ‚â• the current
  holder's; returns a handle. A higher-priority acquire **preempts** the current holder
  (notifies it so it can stop cleanly).
- `setTargets/runSteps(lease, ‚Ä¶)` ‚Äî motion calls now require a valid, current lease;
  a call from a superseded lease is **rejected** (no thrash).
- `release(lease)` ‚Äî give the body back; the next-highest waiter (if any) takes it.
- **LEASE TTL + liveness** ‚Äî a lease **auto-expires** unless renewed (faceFollow renews
  each control tick). So a **crashed/hung follower releases the body within one TTL**
  (not frozen forever) ‚Äî the safety property. Brain/console leases are short-lived
  (a gesture) so they expire naturally.
- **preempt callback** ‚Äî the held loop is told "you've been preempted" so it stops its
  own ticking (faceFollow pauses; doesn't keep computing corrections into the void).

**Backward-compat:** existing callers (brain, console, tasks) wrap in a short
auto-acquire-at-their-priority + release, so today's behaviour is unchanged when
faceFollow isn't running. The lease only *bites* when two holders contend.

**Where it lives:** on/beside `MotionExecutor` in `bodylink/` ‚Äî it IS the body's master,
so arbitration belongs there, mediating every caller (not bolted onto faceFollow).

## 4b. v1 AS-BUILT ‚Äî a plain task, no lease (the simplest path)

Decision (deliberate, scope): **v1 faceFollow is a plain TASK** (`tasks/packaged/
face-follow/`), not the lease/conductor. The follow LOOP genuinely is just a task ‚Äî
`recognize` (now returns the box, ¬ß3a was a 3-line capability add, NOT a new sensor) +
`move`, in a `while` loop. The **lease is deferred** ‚Äî its only job is safe coexistence
with the live brain, which v1 sidesteps by *not running mid-conversation*. ¬ß4's lease
design stands as the "when coexistence matters" plan.

What shipped:
- **`recognize` returns `box {x,y,w,h}`** (option A ‚Äî reuse the existing sensor, +name
  for free; a box-only fast detector is the fallback only if per-tick recognize proves
  too heavy at the rate we want ‚Äî not pre-built).
- **`control.ts` (pure, tested):** `pickTarget` (named/salient + continuity LOCK),
  `nextPose` (proportional pan+tilt, deadband + slew, sign-correct), `sweepStep` (search
  from last-known outward). 16 unit tests.
- **`task.ts`:** the loop ‚Äî recognize ‚Üí pick ‚Üí track (`nextPose`‚Üí`move`) or search-sweep
  when missing; `target?` param (named) else most-salient; checkpoints its pose.
- **NOT in v1:** the lease (¬ß4), ego-motion loop-closure via `pushCommand` (no task
  capability for it yet; the per-tick re-recognize self-corrects a panned-past face).

### Multi-person policy (how a crowd doesn't break it)
`recognize` returns ALL faces. The risk is **oscillation** ‚Äî two people ‚Üí flip-flop. The
fix is **select-one + lock + deliberate switch** (`pickTarget`):
- **named** (`target` set) ‚Üí follow only that recognized person, ignore others;
- else **most-salient** (largest √ó most-central);
- **continuity LOCK** ‚Äî once chosen, keep following *that* person (matched tick-to-tick
  by box proximity), switching only when they leave OR a challenger is **decisively** more
  salient (‚â•1.4√ó ‚Äî hysteresis, same anti-flicker discipline as the identity stream). v1
  locks by **box-continuity** (no recognition dependency); named-lock rides on top.

## 5. Build order (lease-first)

The lease is the risk; prove it before the control math (matches the conductor
¬ß11 spirit):

1. **The lease/arbiter on MotionExecutor** ‚Äî acquire/release/TTL/preempt + priorities;
   wrap existing callers compat. Unit-test the arbitration table + TTL expiry.
2. **Prove the keystone with a TRIVIAL mock follower** ‚Äî a behaviour that just pans
   back-and-forth holding a lease. Verify, on the real body: acquire ‚Üí stream moves ‚Üí
   **kill it ‚Üí body releases within TTL** ‚Üí brain regains control; brain turn **preempts**
   it mid-sweep. *This is the make-or-break test.*
3. **Faster box source** (3a) ‚Äî drop to ~5 Hz; measure jitter.
4. **The real controller** (3b) ‚Äî error‚Üídelta, deadband, slew, the state machine,
   loop-closure. Replaces the mock follower.
5. **Named-target** + tuning on the real dock.

If step 2 is clean, the design holds; if the lease is ugly there, fix it before any
control math rests on it.

## 6. Open points

- **TTL value** ‚Äî short enough that a dead follower frees the body fast (~1‚Äì2 control
  ticks, e.g. 500 ms‚Äì1 s), long enough not to expire mid-track. Tune in step 2.
- **Preempt resume** ‚Äî when the brain releases, does faceFollow resume from where it was

- **The conductor itself** ‚Äî faceFollow is the first action behaviour; whether it runs

## 7. The `perceive` stream ‚Äî on-device MLKit as the fast face source (answers ¬ß3a)

**Why.** The live test (2026-06-25) showed the station-side `recognize` (face-api on a
WebRTC-pulled frame) is the wrong source for tracking: it (a) **misses off-frontal faces**
‚Äî a full right-side sweep at face height detected no one until a frontal face appeared at
the edge ‚Äî and (b) reads the **box `y` far too low** (‚âà0.89 for a face the console centers
at `neck=0`), making the controller dive the neck to its down-limit and stare at the floor.
Neither is a controller bug; both are signal-quality problems in the face-api path.

Meanwhile the **phone already runs MLKit face detection** (`FaceTracker.kt`, ~1 Hz,
`PERFORMANCE_MODE_FAST` + `LANDMARK_MODE_ALL` + `CLASSIFICATION_MODE_ALL` +
`enableTracking`) and emits `FaceSeen(x,y,size)` on a **phone-local** `PerceptionBus` ‚Äî
consumed only for wake/presence/UI, never forwarded. MLKit is on-device (low latency),
mirror-corrected, and **good at off-frontal faces** ‚Äî exactly what tracking needs. Decision
(2026-06-25, with the user): **forward the on-device signal to the station as a new
`perceive` perception stream**, and have faceFollow consume *that* instead of `recognize`.

**Scope (this round):** vision-only (audio VAD/level deferred to its own stream later).
Push **everything MLKit already computes** ‚Äî zero new inference; the cost is serialization
+ one `StationLink.publish` per tick.

**Envelope** (`perceive` topic, dock ‚Üí station, ~1 Hz; payload):
```
{ faces: [ {
    x, y, size,                 // NDC box center (‚àí1..+1, mirror-corrected) + width frac
    bbox: { l, t, r, b },       // full rect, NDC
    yaw, pitch, roll,           // MLKit headEulerAngleY/X/Z ‚Äî head pose ("facing me?")
    trackingId,                 // stable across frames ‚Üí continuity lock (better than proximity)
    smile, leftEyeOpen, rightEyeOpen,   // MLKit probabilities (raw, pre-fusion)
    landmarks: [ { type, x, y } ]       // up to 11 points, NDC
  } ],
  emotion: { kind, confidence },        // FER+ fusion (already emitted)
  gesture: { name, palm, score } | null,// MediaPipe (already emitted)
  identity: { name, confidence } | null,// on-device (already emitted)
  zoom: { ratio, min, max }             // CameraX zoomState ‚Äî current FRAMING (free, this build)
}
```

**Zoom vs Focus ‚Äî two separate camera levers (2026-06-25).** They are distinct concerns:
- **Zoom = framing** (field of view). CameraX `cameraControl.setZoomRatio` +
  `cameraInfo.zoomState`, free off the handle we already hold (`bindToLifecycle`). Digital
  only on this front cam ‚Äî crops+upscales the already-small (320√ó240) analysis frame, so it
  helps *framing/centering* a distant subject but does **not** recover real detail at distance.
- **Focus = fidelity** (dynamic resolution): run analysis/vision at low res when nothing
  needs detail (cheap/battery), raise it on demand ("read that label", enroll a face). The
  seams already exist ‚Äî `FaceTracker.bind` pins analysis at 320√ó240 (with a comment that
  recognition reliability is bound to it), `encodeJpegBase64` caps the vision JPEG at
  320px/q70, and `captureRecognitionJpegBase64` already takes a 640√ó480 on-demand still. A
  `focus.level` of `rough | normal | detail` would map to concrete (analysisRes, jpegMaxEdge,
  quality) tuples ‚Äî the brain/behaviours drive intent, not pixels.

**This build carries `zoom` read-state only.** The focus/resolution signal is **deferred**
until the rough/normal/detail levels are designed (raising res has real ISP/CPU cost on the
2018 phone ‚Äî the contention the FaceTracker comments warn of ‚Äî and recognition thresholds
were tuned at 320px, so a raise needs re-validation). The **write** sides ‚Äî `set_zoom` and
`set_focus` brain tools (the first *downward* camera controls) ‚Äî are both follow-ups.

**Dynamic resolution ‚Äî safe to build, deferred to its own pass (2026-06-25).** Verified the
station does NOT bake in a fixed resolution: `frame-grabber.ts` parses W/H from the VP8
keyframe and `recognizer.ts` normalizes boxes by actual W/H ‚Üí coords are 0..1 regardless of
res. So changing res won't break downstream geometry. The ONE real dependency is that
recognition match thresholds were *tuned* at 320px crops (a raise shifts embedding distances
‚Üí re-validation, not a crash). Safe half (lower fidelity for cheap mode; use the EXISTING
on-demand 640 still for "detail" without renegotiating the live stream) is reliable; raising
the live analysis res is the part needing re-tuning. Deferred whole to keep this build
focused on landing the follow.

**Noise control on the stream (REQUIRED, in the forwarder).** ~1 Hz raw + a few-% box
jitter on a still face would spam the bus/console. The forwarder dedups: **change-significant
+ rate-limited + heartbeat** (same shape as the identity stream's CONFIRM/DROP hysteresis):
suppress a frame ~identical to the last sent (Œîx/Œîy/Œîsize < Œµ, same trackingId + emotion +
gesture + identity); coalesce fast change to a min-interval (‚â§ ~5 Hz); always send
transitions (face appear/disappear, identity change, new trackingId) immediately; and send a
heartbeat at most every ~2‚Äì3 s when unchanged so the station knows the face is *still there*
(not stale). Dedup lives in the forwarder (one place), not the detector path.

**Live test 2 (2026-06-25, build 14 OTA'd to dock-redmi).** The perceive stream worked
end-to-end: rich frames (box + Euler + trackingId + landmarks + emotion + zoom) flowed, and
the **eye-midpoint anchor fixed the neck-dive** ‚Äî a centered face now reads y‚âà0.44 (the eyes)
instead of ‚âà0.89 (the sagging box), so the head centers and holds level instead of diving to
the floor. faceFollow acquired, centered, and held on a still person. **New problem found:**
MLKit detection is **bursty** ‚Äî it drops a still, frontal, *centered* face for multiple
seconds at a time (observed `/perceive` going blank for 7‚Äì10 s between detection bursts).
The old fixed `lostAfter` grace (~8 ticks) then expired and the head **swept away from a
person who never moved** ("it moved, I didn't"). Fix: **asymmetric grace** ‚Äî when the lock
was CENTERED at drop-out, wait `lostAfterCentered` (default 4√ó `lostAfter`) and keep looking
*right here* before concluding they left and sweeping; a centered person doesn't teleport, so
a gap is a detection dropout, not a departure. `lock.centered` carries the "were we holding
ON them" bit; both tunable as task params. Encoded as a closed-loop test (centered drop-out ‚Üí
holds still, never sweeps, re-locks on return). The deeper detection sparsity is the
remaining real limit (a faster/steadier on-device detector, or fusing the burst with a
predicted hold, is future work).

**faceFollow is a BEHAVIOUR, with cooldown-on-yield (2026-06-25).** Reframed from a one-shot
task to a **standing behaviour** (user): it never completes ‚Äî find ‚Üí follow ‚Üí hold when still
‚Üí sweep when lost ‚Üí forever. It only **yields** the body when another mover takes it, then
**cools down** before resuming. This is the actuator-lease idea in its lightest form ‚Äî observe
who's driving, defer, don't hard-lock:
- **Move-source tagging.** `MotionExecutor` now records `lastMover {tag, at}` per dock; every
  command path passes a source ‚Äî `brain-turn` (the brain's move/gesture tools), `console`,
  `task:<instanceId>` (the `move` capability stamps the calling task), `station`. New read
  capability `bodyMover` returns it.
- **Yield + cooldown.** Each tick faceFollow reads `bodyMover`; a tag that isn't its own
  `task:<id>` and newer than its last move = a FOREIGN mover ‚Üí it stops commanding and arms a
  `cooldownMs` window (default **60 s**, "we'll adjust"; `FF_COOLDOWN_MS` / task param). While
  cooling it issues nothing (won't fight) but keeps the lock, so it resumes on the same person.
  A foreign move during cooldown re-arms the full window. **Cold start never yields** ‚Äî the
  cooldown only triggers on a real foreign move. Pure + injected-clock tested (cold-start
  immediate, yield, hold-through-cooldown, resume, re-yield).

**Adaptive on-device detection cadence (2026-06-25).** The live bottleneck was perceive-stream
sparsity (~0.3‚Äì1 Hz): MLKit ran at a fixed 1 Hz on the phone and the forwarder deduped further.
Fixed-rate constants are guesses that rot (different phone, lighting, CPU load), so instead of
re-tuning, `FaceTracker` now runs the face path at an **adaptive interval**: it measures each
run's full cost (encode + async detect + FER, via `addOnCompleteListener`) as an EMA and sets
the next gate to `cost √ó SLACK`, clamped ~5 Hz‚Ä¶2 Hz. A fast phone runs fast; a loaded/slow
phone self-limits ‚Äî no per-device rate to tune. (Build 15.)

**`objects` ‚Äî reserved, not built (2026-06-25).** MLKit *can* detect objects, but via a
SEPARATE model (`object-detection`: boxes + tracking + only 5 coarse categories) ‚Äî a NEW
analyzer pass = real CPU/ISP cost on the 2018 phone, NOT free like the face extras. Object
*naming* is already the vision sidecar's job (Qwen-VL, open-vocabulary, far better than
MLKit's 5 labels) ‚Äî just slower. The envelope reserves room for a future `objects: [...]`
array (fast on-device boxes/motion the slow sidecar can't give), but adding it is a deliberate
cost/value call for its own pass; phone stays face-only here.

**What the 11 landmarks reliably buy us** (computed server-side, so we iterate without a
re-flash): **eye-midpoint anchor** ‚Äî center the head on the eyes, not the box geometric
center (the box center is what reads low; the eye midpoint is stable) ‚Äî this is the direct
fix for the neck-dive; a **gaze-on-dock** boolean (yaw within ¬±N¬∞ AND eyes open) for a
future "follow whoever's attending to me" salient-pick; and a **relative** distance proxy
(inter-ocular px). NOT reliable from 11 coarse points: precise eye gaze, absolute distance,
viseme/talking, micro-expression ‚Äî don't build behaviour on those.

**Cannot get cheaply (excluded):** full-body pose / hand skeleton joints / depth / iris-gaze
/ age ‚Äî all need new models on a 2018-class phone. "body-track" here means **head
orientation** (Euler), not a body-pose model.

**Plumbing.** The high-rate face geometry is a **live latest-state** signal (faceFollow reads
"the last face frame"), NOT the heavy 1000-cap `SnapshotStore` memory ring ‚Äî only
emotion/identity (kinds the ring already models) optionally also land as snapshots.
faceFollow's `recognize`-based `faces()` is replaced by a read of the per-dock latest
`perceive` faces; the box‚Üíerror math is unchanged (it just gets a better box, and later the
eye-midpoint). Use of `yaw/pitch/roll` in the controller is **transport-now, use-later** (the
data flows + is visible; salient-by-facing is a follow-up).

## Learnings (the iteration, captured ‚Äî these were expensive to earn)

faceFollow took many live-hardware rounds to get stable. The lessons generalize to any
embodied control task on this platform ‚Äî capture them so we don't re-pay:

1. **Physical servos + bursty perception are flaky by nature.** A single run proves nothing
   (good OR bad) ‚Äî a "follow" that worked once could be luck; a failure could be a servo
   hiccup or a detection blink. Judge **aggregate behaviour over time** ("kept them in view
   most of the time, recovered"), never a single tick. The design bar is *robustness to its
   own flakiness*, not precision.

2. **Don't grow the step on a stale error.** The first controller GREW its step when the
   error didn't shrink ("push harder"). But the error didn't shrink because **detection lags
   the head** (a stale read) ‚Äî so it accelerated and flung the head ~56¬∞ *past* the person,
   turning the camera off them ‚Üí blind ‚Üí "it moves away." The fix: **STEP-AND-SETTLE** ‚Äî
   small FIXED step toward the face's *side* (sign only, never the laggy magnitude), then
   HOLD a tick so a fresh look lands before deciding again. Small+patient converges;
   big+eager overshoots.

3. **Trust only signals you can actually get.** An algorithm that *sounds* right but needs a
   signal you can't reliably read is worthless. We could NOT reliably tell "lost the face
   because I moved" from "detection just blinked" ‚Äî they look identical ‚Äî so any rule keyed
   on that distinction was unimplementable. Build on **presence + sign** (trusted), not on
   exact position (laggy) or loss-attribution (unknowable).

4. **Instrument, don't theorize ‚Äî and trust the right source of truth.** Weeks of "it
   doesn't see me / it moves on" were resolved only by (a) **WS telemetry** from the phone
   (framesIn / detector-passes / hits / lastFace ‚Üí pinpointed camera-vs-gate-vs-detector vs
   "you weren't actually in front") and (b) an **on-device indicator** the user could watch
   (LOCK/TRACK/WAIT/SEARCH). The indicator turned out *more* reliable than reading logs ‚Äî so
   "can't blame the detector" became provable. Reading the station log tick-by-tick and
   declaring "it works" repeatedly burned trust; the lived behaviour + ground-truth
   telemetry are the arbiters, not a hopeful log line.

5. **"Lock and wait" vs "search" is a real tension ‚Äî tune the hold, don't pick a pole.**
   Holding a lost lock forever means it stares at an empty spot; searching immediately means
   it abandons a person through a normal detection blink. The answer is a **bounded hold**
   (~30s): ride out dropouts, then give up and sweep. (We over-corrected to ~forever first.)

6. **Eye-midpoint, not box center.** The face box's geometric center reads LOW (the box sags
