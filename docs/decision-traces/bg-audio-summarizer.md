## 0. TL;DR

Today's `background-stt` is a Gemini flash-lite call that **re-transcribes** each
VAD-endpointed *speech* utterance into diarized text for the recall record. On
real data it is **not earning its place**: 81% of long-term memories are filed
under a meaningless `speaker 0` subject, the diarization has produced exactly one
(garbage) second-speaker attribution ever, and it sometimes replaces an honest
local transcript with a confident-but-wrong Gemini one.

The reframe: stop treating the sensor as a transcriber. Treat it as a
**background audio processor** that answers *"what just happened acoustically, and
how significant is it?"* — speech OR music OR a crash OR laughter OR crying —
each with a **confidence score**. It runs only on **significant activity**, sends
the **last N seconds** (default 10s, configurable) of audio, and outputs a rich
schema (`kind` / `transcript` / `affect` / `intensity` / `salience` / `summary`).

**Decision-making (respond / ask / be curious) stays PARKED in the brain.** This
processor produces observations; the brain owns reactions.

The plan splits by what the evidence supports: **`kind` + `salience` + transcript
ship now** (well-supported); **acoustic `affect` is captured but NOT trusted**
until benchmarked on our own room audio, because external benchmarks put
flash-class models at ~chance on wordless emotional sound.

## 1. Why — the live data verdict on today's pipeline

Pulled from the running station (`dock-redmi`) — the persisted memory store and
live snapshots, not theory:

- **575** long-term memories; **465 (81%)** reference a numbered speaker; **456**
  have their *subject* literally set to `"speaker 0"`. The single most common
  "person" in the robot's memory is a meaningless label.
- **Exactly 1** memory ever invoked `speaker 1` — and it's junk ("Speaker 1
  observed that the robot reported having moved"). Only two indices (S0, S1) were
  ever assigned. There is functionally **no diarization** happening.
- Average confidence of speaker memories: **0.48** — the curator itself is unsure.
- The re-transcription also *degrades*: raw local STT "Oh yeah," / "Yeah." / "Yeah,
  I think it's" all got rewritten by Gemini to an identical, wrong **"I don't
  know"**; "Hey, the light number." → "Yeah I think it's" (a swap with a different
  utterance). Confident hallucination replacing rough-but-honest text.

**Root cause of the diarization failure is structural, not tunable:** we send
Gemini **one isolated clip** with no prior audio, no voiceprint, no enrollment
(see [`background-stt.ts:79-88`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/background-stt.ts#L79-L88)).
`S1` in one clip has no defined relationship to `S1` in the next, so per-utterance
integer speaker indices can never be stable. For a mostly single-user desk
companion, the prompt's main job is to *suppress* false second speakers — we pay
for machinery whose job is to not fire.

**Frequency today:** one Gemini call **per VAD-endpointed utterance**, with **no
throttle, dedup, or confidence gate**. The hallucination/backchannel filters
([`stt-watch.ts:643-644`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/stt-watch.ts#L643-L644))
gate only whether it becomes a *brain turn* — they do NOT gate the Gemini call.
Every "yeah"/"uh-huh" was billed and re-transcribed. Cost is low in absolute terms
(~$3/mo, [recall-reliability](../findings/recall-reliability.md)) only because the
dock is idle most of the time — the local VAD `SILENCE_RMS` gate is the sole cost
brake.

## 2. The reframe — auditory scene interpreter

The job changes from "what words were said" to "what is this sound, and how
significant is it?". `kind` and `affect` are **separate axes** (shouting-in-joy vs
shouting-in-anger share a `kind` but differ in `affect`).

Proposed per-event output schema (every field carries its own confidence):

```
kind:       speech | laughter | crying | shouting | music | impact | alarm | ambient | animal | unknown   (+conf)
transcript: <words if speech; often "" for laughter/crying/impact>   (+conf, from the LOCAL engine)
affect:     calm | happy | excited | angry | sad | distressed | fearful | amused | pained | ...   (+conf)
intensity:  mild | moderate | intense           // how MUCH affect, orthogonal to which
salience:   low | notable | startling           // "would a human's head turn?"
summary:    "Two people talking over music; one sounds excited."   (+conf)
window_ms:  how much audio this covers (so downstream knows it's an N-second window)
```

### Emotion is ACOUSTIC, not speech-emotion

Affect is a property of the **sound**, independent of words — laughter, crying,
screaming, a groan, a gasp have no transcript. So `affect` is a **top-level field
on every event**, not a sub-field of the speech branch. This is *cleaner* than
speech-emotion (which needs words and breaks on non-speech), and it captures the
highest-salience events an embodied companion could want (someone crying nearby),
which a words-only pipeline is structurally blind to.

### Trigger vs. payload — the key structural change

Two asks that seem to conflict ("run only on significant activity" vs "send the
last 10s") are compatible **only if trigger and payload are separated**:

- **Trigger** = "did something significant happen?" — energy onset / spectral
  novelty / voiced speech. Cheap, local, no LLM.
- **Payload** = the **last N seconds of a continuous, never-drained ring buffer**
  ending at the trigger. A crash at t=0 sends t−10 → t, giving Gemini the lead-up
  (was there talking? music that stopped?) — which is what makes "what happened /
  was this startling" answerable.

Today the buffer is *drained* at each speech endpoint. The reframe keeps a rolling
ring that the trigger *snapshots*; the VAD endpoint becomes just one kind of
trigger among several.

### Debounce is the cost knob, not a nicety

"Buffer 10s + fire on activity" is a cost multiplier if rapid triggers send
overlapping windows. A **debounce/cooldown** (at most one window per K seconds
unless salience jumps) is what makes the net-cost direction favorable. See §5.

## 3. Evidence — external benchmarks (does this even work?)

The reframe's riskiest claim is "read affect from wordless sound with flash-lite."
The literature says: **be careful — that specific claim is near-chance today.**

Primary source: **["Do Audio LLMs Really LISTEN, or Just Transcribe?"](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.10444v1)**
(arXiv 2510.10444) — isolates *acoustic* emotion from *lexical* emotion.

| Condition | Gemini 2.5-Pro | Gemini 2.5-Flash | Random |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acoustic emotion, neutral words (audio-only) | 34.9% | **25.6%** | 12.5% |
| **Paralinguistic — pure wordless sound (laughter/sighs)** | 15.7% | **18.0%** | 12.5% |

- **Wordless emotional sound is ~chance** — and this is *Flash*; we run the weaker
  *flash-lite*.
- Documented failure modes: strong **"neutral" bias** (defaults to neutral,
  ignores tone); **lexical dominance** (when tone and words conflict, models follow
  the words — they detect "something's off" but can't name it); **paralinguistic
  incompetence** (non-verbal sound approaches chance).

Corroborating the *other* half (sound-**kind** classification, which the reframe
depends on more):
- Gemini 2.5-Flash ≈ **75%** on audio intent classification ([MSEB](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.04556));
  the Gemini report notes it "might make mistakes recognizing sound that's not
  speech." So **sound-kind is decent; acoustic affect is near-chance.**
- Flash-Lite is ~half the cost of Flash and "at or near Flash quality on routine
  classification/extraction" ([pricing/tradeoff refs](https://blog.laozhang.ai/en/posts/gemini-3-5-flash-vs-gemini-3-1-flash-lite)),
  but the affect gap should be assumed to persist.

**Conclusion:** `kind` + `salience` are supported; `affect` is not, yet. The 10s
window is one of the few levers that might lift affect above chance (paralinguistic
scores are on short clips), which is an independent reason to build the ring buffer.

### 4.1 The two curator landmines (HIGH — invisible from the surface)

These are the findings that most change the plan. **The reframe is pointless
without fixing both**, and neither is obvious:

1. **`toObservation` silently drops every wordless sound.**
   [`sources.ts:105`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/memory/longterm/sources.ts#L105):
   `if (!text || text.replace(/[^a-z0-9]/gi,'').length < 2) return null`. A
   crying/laughter/alarm snapshot has `text:""` but rich `kind`/`affect` → returns
   null → never becomes an Observation → never reaches consolidate. The whole point
   ("remember the baby cried for 20 min") fails here. **Must widen to admit
   affect-bearing wordless records.**

2. **Watermark-vs-null-filter stall / re-scan.**
   [`pendingStats`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/memory/longterm/sources.ts#L85)
   counts every post-watermark speech snapshot (incl. wordless), but
   `pendingObservations` null-filters them, and the watermark advances only to the
   last *non-null* observation ([`curator.ts:197`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/memory/longterm/curator.ts#L197)).
   An all-wordless batch → `obs.length===0` → `consolidate` returns early
   ([`curator.ts:178`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/memory/longterm/curator.ts#L178))
   **without advancing the watermark** → the curator re-scans the same span every
   tick forever. Acute once the new gate produces many wordless snapshots. **The
   watermark must advance over scanned-but-skipped records.**

### 4.2 The `emotion`-kind collision (turn it into an asset)

An `emotion` snapshot kind + processor **already exists** —
[`snapshots.ts:33`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/snapshots.ts#L33),
[`identity-snapshot.ts:134`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/identity-snapshot.ts#L134) —
producing **visual** affect from face-api (the face you *see*). Acoustic `affect`
is a **different sensor for the same concept**. Do NOT naively reuse the `emotion`
kind (it would corrupt the summarizer's tallies at
[`summarizer.ts:194,211-213`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/summarizer.ts#L194)).
Instead, **fuse them**: voice-says-distressed AND face-says-sad is corroboration
neither near-chance signal gives alone. Given §3, this face+voice cross-check is
probably the *only* path to trustworthy affect — and half of it is already built.

## 5. Cost model

Cost path: [`background-stt.ts:104`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/background-stt.ts#L104)
→ `reportGeminiCost(dockId, model, 'bg-stt', …)` → [`cost-report.ts`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/cost-report.ts)
(flash-lite: audio-in **$0.30/1M**, priced 3× text-in; audio dominates).

**The ~$3/mo anchor is NOT valid for the reframe.** Two forces push cost up:
10s windows are **~2-4× more audio per call** than today's 1-6s utterances, and the
new gate fires on events today's speech-VAD ignores (crying, music, alarms). So:

```
$/mo ≈ trigger_rate × 10s_audio_tokens × $0.30/1M
```

The **debounce cooldown is the only clamp** on `trigger_rate` — it is the primary
cost lever, not a detail. Gate + debounce well → *fewer, richer* calls (kills the
per-grunt spam) → plausibly *cheaper* than today. Gate poorly → 10s on every sound
→ a real bill. Model this explicitly before shipping.

## 6. Plan (phased by evidence)

### Phase 1 — the evidence-backed core (safe to build)
1. Rename `background-stt` → `background-audio-processor`; env
   `PERCEPTION_BG_STT_MODEL` → `PERCEPTION_BG_AUDIO_MODEL` (alias old for
   back-compat); cost label `bg-stt` → `bg-audio`; `/bg-stt` route.
2. Widen schema to **`kind` + `salience` + `transcript`**, each with confidence.
   Transcript confidence comes from the **local engine** (real logprob/no-speech,
   the existing `confTier`), NOT Gemini's self-report — tag sources so a vibe isn't
   mistaken for a probability.
3. **Ring buffer** — continuous never-drained N-second PCM ring (default 10s,
   `PERCEPTION_BG_WINDOW_MS`). Template already exists: `#preroll`
   ([`stt-watch.ts:168,271-273`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/stt-watch.ts#L168)).
4. **Debounce/cooldown** (`PERCEPTION_BG_COOLDOWN_MS`) with an in-flight guard.
   Template: `#lastInterimMs`/`#interimInFlight`
   ([`stt-watch.ts:178-179,285-294`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/stt-watch.ts#L178)).
5. **Energy-onset trigger** (extend the RMS already at
   [`stt-watch.ts:251-253`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/stt-watch.ts#L251));
   **defer spectral novelty** — there is no FFT anywhere in the file; it is net-new
   DSP and not needed for v1.
6. **Fix the two curator landmines** (§4.1) — mandatory, or wordless events vanish
   and the curator thrashes.

Phase 1 alone kills the "speaker 0" pollution and captures sound events — all of it
evidence-supported.

### Phase 2 — affect, gated behind proof (do NOT ship on faith)
7. Add `affect` + `intensity` as **experimental, memory-only** fields. Show in the
   Studio, let them accumulate, but **the brain must not act on them.**
8. **Run a ~20-clip flash-lite bench on our OWN room audio** (laugh, cry, shout,
   neutral talk, music, a bang) before trusting affect — the external ~18% is on
   curated data; ours will likely be worse. Fits the
   [`models/BENCHMARKS.md`](../../orbit-station/models/BENCHMARKS.md) pattern. Test
   both window lengths (the 10s window is the lever that might lift affect).
9. **Fuse acoustic affect with the existing visual `emotion` kind** (§4.2) — the
   face+voice cross-check is likely the only route to trustworthy affect.

### Invariant across both phases
- **Decision-making stays in the brain.** `onFinal`
  ([`stt-watch.ts:643-650`](../../orbit-station/server/src/modules/perception/processors/stt-watch.ts#L643))
  is already independent of the bg call (fire-and-forget `void backgroundStt(...)`).
  Keep it that way — do not route `affect`/`kind` into `onFinal` to make wordless
  sounds addressable; that crosses the boundary this reframe deliberately preserves.

## 7a. Review addendum (2026-07-05, fresh-eyes pass)

> Reviewed against the live codebase after the idle-moods / attention-director work.
> **Verdict: the reframe is right, the evidence discipline is right — approve Phase 1
> with the four additions below folded in.** The strongest parts stand: the live-data
> indictment (§1), affect gated behind an own-audio bench (§3/§6-Phase-2), decisions
> staying in the brain, and the two curator landmines (§4.1 — landmine 2, the watermark
> stall, deserves a check TODAY: low-confidence filler that null-filters may already be
> stalling the curator occasionally, independent of this reframe).

1. **The pollution stops but is never cleaned.** Phase 1 prevents new "speaker 0"
   subjects; the 456 existing memories stay polluted and keep surfacing in recall. Add a
   one-off migration (the curator's `revise`/`reconcile` op is the tool): re-subject or
   demote every `speaker N`-keyed memory. Without it the headline symptom survives the fix.
2. **Coordinate with the addressed-vs-overheard seam (TODO §3.0) — same record, same
   patch mechanism.** The planned `payload.addressed` back-patch from the brain writes
   into the SAME speech snapshot via the SAME `stt-watch` patch block this reframe
   rewrites. Land them together, and put `addressed` in the event schema — "speech,
   addressed to the dock" vs "speech, ambient" is worth more to memory quality than
   diarization ever was, and the brain already computes it for free.
3. **Spec the non-speech triggers or drop the claim.** V1's energy-onset trigger fires on
   roughly what the speech-VAD already fires on — so v1 is "same call pattern, richer
   output" (fine, but say so). The genuinely-new detections (crying, music, a crash) need
   two envelope shapes the doc should pin down: SUSTAINED non-VAD energy (music) and
   IMPULSE onset (crash) — both RMS-derivable, no FFT.
4. **Gate the expensive call on CHANGE, never slow the sensing** (lesson from the vision
   pipeline the same day: a doubling-pause design was rejected because it can sleep
   through significant events; the shipped design probes cheaply at full rate and gates
   only the inference). Audio equivalent: the cheap trigger/RMS runs continuously;
   consecutive windows classifying identically (music playing for an hour → same
   kind+summary) lengthen only the Gemini COOLDOWN, and any trigger-shape change resets
   it. Also right-size the cost fear: even 1 call/min 24/7 at flash-lite audio pricing is
   ~$4/mo — the debounce matters more for snapshot-ring noise and curator load than
   dollars.

Smaller notes: (a) naming — prefer the doc's "background-audio-processor / auditory scene
interpreter" over "summarizer" (it classifies + scores; the summary is one field); rename
this file to match when the code moves. (b) The deferred real-time question has a named
answer: when reaction time comes, `salience` feeds the attention gate (`evaluateGate`) —
NOT `onFinal` — which keeps §6's invariant intact. (c) Phase-2 fusion caveat: the visual
`emotion` stream only exists while a face is in view, so out-of-view acoustic affect
(crying in the next room) stays uncorroborated — keep it untrusted unless intensity is
high AND repeated across windows.

## 7. Open questions

- **Real-time vs recall-only.** Examples like "is someone calling me?" sound
  real-time, but today this is the *non-blocking recall* path, separate from the
  live turn. Real-time reaction means feeding `salience`/`addressed` into the live
  addressing loop — a bigger wire-up than patching a stored snapshot. **Deferred
  with the decision layer.**
- **Debounce policy** — fixed cooldown, or salience-adaptive (a `startling` event
  bypasses the cooldown)? Sizes the cost.
- **`kind`/`affect` taxonomy** — the lists above are a starting point; pin down
  before the prompt is written.
- **Model tier** — 2.5 flash-lite today; a 3.x flash tier reportedly improves audio
  understanding, but assume the affect gap persists until our own bench says
  otherwise.
